### Why `text_en` is null everywhere (honest scope limit)

The design intent was Griffith's public-domain English. On inspection Griffith **cannot be aligned to the shloka grid**, so `text_en` is left `null` for all 18761 records (English coverage **0.00%**) rather than fabricating verse boundaries:

1. **No per-shloka units.** Griffith is a *poetic rhyming-couplet* translation printed as continuous verse under per-CANTO headings, with no shloka numbers or markers. Its opening couplets -- "To sainted Narad, prince of those / Whose lore in words of wisdom flows. / Whose constant care and chief delight / Were Scripture and ascetic rite, / The good Valmiki..." -- already fuse and reorder Baroda shlokas 1.1.1-1.1.4. Per-shloka boundaries are unrecoverable.
2. **Canto != sarga.** Griffith's cantos do not map 1:1 onto the Baroda critical-edition sargas (different recension and editorial canto splits).
3. **Book 7 untranslated.** Griffith never translated the Uttara Kanda (kanda 7) at all.
Griffith landing pages are cached under `raw/griffith/` for provenance only. A future revision could attach Griffith at *canto* granularity (coarser than the shloka ID space) or substitute another verse-aligned public-domain prose translation (e.g. M. N. Dutt, 1891); neither is done here to keep the released index strictly non-fabricated.

### Edition note: counts vs the popular ~24,000-shloka figure

This corpus is the **Baroda Critical Edition** (18761 shlokas, 606 sargas). The widely-quoted "~24,000 shlokas" refers to the longer Northern/vulgate recension (e.g. Gita Press, with interpolations the critical edition relegates to appendices). The critical edition is the scholarly standard and is what GRETIL's Tokunaga file encodes; all ids here are real and citable in that edition.

## Flagged / notes

- **English (all records):** `text_en=null`, `translation_source=null`, `source_urls.en=null` -- Griffith not shloka-alignable (see above). This is the single, global, intentional gap.
- **`tokens`** counts whitespace tokens of the **IAST transliteration** (the available citable text), since `text_en` is null. This differs from the Gita build, where `tokens` counted English words.
- **Devanagari** is a deterministic transliteration of the GRETIL IAST, joined as `pada-a । pada-c ॥` (danda between half-verses, double danda at end). Some shlokas have extra half-verses (padas `e`/`g`) -> additional `।`-separated segments; a few sarga-final shlokas are a single half-verse.
- No sarga gaps were detected in any kanda (see table); all sargas run contiguously 1..max.
